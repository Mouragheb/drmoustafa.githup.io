          <h4 >About him:</h2>

          <li>He was born December 25th 1920.</li>

          <li>Was an Egyptian doctor and a prolific author.</li>

          <li>Moustafa Mahmoud was born in Shibin el-Kom, Munufiyya province, Egypt.</li>

          <li>He was trained as a doctor, but later chose a career as a journalist and author.</li>

          <li>He was traveling widely and writing on many subjects.</li>

          <li>Moustafa Mahmud describes his frequent travels, starting with his journeys to the Tropics in Tanzania, Uganda, Kenya, and the south of Sudan, where he stayed for two months with the tribe of Niam Niam.</li>

          <li>He also traveled to the Sahara Desert, to the oasis of Ghadamis, where he stayed for a month with the tribe of El Tawariq. Furthermore, he traveled to many capitals of European and American countries, such as: Italy, Germany, Greece, France, Canada and the United States; and to the Arab countries, beginning with Morocco and Algeria in the west; and ending with Lebanon, Syria and Saudi Arabia in the east.</li>

          <li>He wrote five books criticizing the Marxist thought: Islamic left Fib; Marxism and Islam; Leftism Collapse; Why Did I Refuse Marxism?; and The Antichrist.</li> 

          <li>He has seventy-five books published, six of them were adapted for stage: (Earthquake; Man and Shade; The Great Alexander; The Social Gang (Shilla-t 'Uns); Blood Odor; The Devil Lives in our House), one of them (The Impossible) was presented as a film; twenty-five books deal with Islamic subjects; and the rest consists of studies and short stories.</li>

          <li>TV presented for him more than four hundred parts of the program "Science and Faith", in which the movie, scientific substance and Sufi meditation guide us to the faith in God.</li> 

          <li>Dr. Moustafa Mahmoud was discovered unconscious on November 1st 2009, despite medics trying to resuscitate him he was already dead. He died at the approximate age of 88.
